overview

Patient Services Intern works under the supervision of healthcare administrators and patient service coordinators in hospitals, clinics, or healthcare organizations. This internship provides valuable experience in patient care, administrative tasks, and customer service in a healthcare setting.

responsiblites

  • Patient Reception: Greet and assist patients as they arrive, ensuring a welcoming environment and addressing any immediate concerns.
  • Appointment Scheduling: Help schedule patient appointments, manage calendars, and confirm upcoming visits.
  • Data Entry: Assist in entering patient information into electronic health records (EHR) and updating existing records for accuracy.
  • Insurance Verification: Support the verification of patient insurance coverage and eligibility, ensuring proper documentation is maintained.
  • Patient Education: Provide information to patients about services, procedures, and available resources, guiding them through the healthcare process.
  • Administrative Support: Perform various administrative tasks, such as filing, photocopying, and preparing patient charts.
  • Collaboration: Work closely with healthcare staff, including nurses and physicians, to ensure coordinated patient care and communication.

Qaulifications

  • Education: Enrollment in or completion of a degree program in healthcare administration, public health, or a related field.
  • Experience: Previous customer service or administrative experience in a healthcare setting is beneficial but not always required.

Skills

  • Clinical Skills: Basic knowledge of physical therapy techniques, anatomy, and rehabilitation protocols.
  • Communication: Strong verbal and written communication skills for interacting effectively with patients and healthcare providers.
  • Empathy and Compassion: Ability to provide emotional support and encouragement to patients during their recovery process.
  • Attention to Detail: Careful monitoring of patient progress and adherence to treatment protocols.
  • Teamwork: Ability to work collaboratively within a healthcare team and follow instructions from supervising therapists.

Learning Opportunities

  • Gain exposure to various areas of physical therapy, such as orthopedics, neurology, pediatrics, and sports rehabilitation.
  • Participate in training sessions on therapeutic techniques, patient assessment, and evidence-based practices.
  • Opportunities to observe and learn from experienced physical therapists during evaluations and treatment sessions.

Career Advancement

Completing a patient service internship is a crucial step toward becoming a licensed physical therapist. It provides valuable experience that can lead to full-time positions in clinics, hospitals, or specialized rehabilitation settings.
