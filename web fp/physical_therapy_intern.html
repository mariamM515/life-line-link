Overview

A Physical Therapy Intern works under the supervision of licensed physical therapists in various settings, such as hospitals, outpatient clinics, rehabilitation centers, or sports facilities. This internship provides hands-on experience in patient care, therapeutic techniques, and rehabilitation processes.

Responsibilities

  • Patient Interaction: Assist in welcoming and preparing patients for therapy sessions, including explaining treatment plans and answering questions.
  • Therapeutic Exercises: Support physical therapists in administering therapeutic exercises, monitoring patient progress, and ensuring proper technique.
  • Mobility Assistance: Help patients with mobility exercises, transfers, and gait training to enhance their physical function.
  • Equipment Setup: Prepare and maintain therapy equipment and assist with modalities such as ultrasound, electrical stimulation, and hot/cold packs.
  • Documentation: Assist in recording patient assessments, progress notes, and treatment outcomes in electronic health records (EHR).
  • Patient Education: Educate patients about home exercise programs and self-care strategies to promote recovery.
  • Collaboration: Work with physical therapists and other healthcare professionals to develop and implement individualized treatment plans.

Qualifications

  • Education: Enrollment in or completion of a physical therapy program (DPT or equivalent).
  • Certification: CPR and First Aid certification may be required.
  • Experience: Previous coursework or clinical rotations in physical therapy is beneficial.

Skills

  • Clinical Skills: Basic knowledge of physical therapy techniques, anatomy, and rehabilitation protocols.
  • Communication: Strong verbal and written communication skills for interacting effectively with patients and healthcare providers.
  • Empathy and Compassion: Ability to provide emotional support and encouragement to patients during their recovery process.
  • Attention to Detail: Careful monitoring of patient progress and adherence to treatment protocols.
  • Teamwork: Ability to work collaboratively within a healthcare team and follow instructions from supervising therapists.

Learning Opportunities

  • Gain exposure to various areas of physical therapy, such as orthopedics, neurology, pediatrics, and sports rehabilitation.
  • Participate in training sessions on therapeutic techniques, patient assessment, and evidence-based practices.
  • Opportunities to observe and learn from experienced physical therapists during evaluations and treatment sessions.

Career Advancement

Completing a physical therapy internship is a crucial step toward becoming a licensed physical therapist. It provides valuable experience that can lead to full-time positions in clinics, hospitals, or specialized rehabilitation settings.
